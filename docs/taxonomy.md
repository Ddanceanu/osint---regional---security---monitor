# Taxonomy

## MVP thematic taxonomy

The first version of the project uses a compact thematic taxonomy meant to support structured classification and simple analytical comparison sources and time periods.

The taxonomy is intentionally limited. The goal of the MVP is not to capture every possible nuance, but to define a stable set of themes that can support filtering, trend analysis, and thematic grouping.

## Main themes

### 1. Support for Ukraine
Documents related to political, institutional, diplomatic, financial, or strategic support for Ukraine.

### 2. Eastern Flank / NATO Deterrence
Documents focused on NATO posture, deterrence measures, reassurance, military presence, exercises, and eastern flank dynamics.

### 3.  Romania - Republic of Moldova
Documents centered on bilateral cooperation, political support, institutional relations, resilience, and European orientation in the Romania-Moldova relationship.

### 4. Russia: Actions, Positioning, and Regional Implications
Documents related to Russia actions, official positioning, pressure, reactions, or broader regional implications connected to Russia.

### 5. EU and Regional Security
Documents focused on the European Union as a security and political actor in relation on the region, including decisions, statements, strategic priorities, and institutional positioning.

### 6. Black Sea and Regional Security
Documents related to the Black Sea area as a strategic space, including regional developments, security relevance, and associated geopolitical concerns.

### 7. Others / Mixed
Documents that do not fit clearly into one primary theme, or that combine multiple themes without a dominant analytic category.

## Classification notes
Each document should receive:
- one main theme;
- optionally one or more secundary themes.

This allows the system to preserve a dominant analytical label while still reflecting overlap between topics.

## Why this taxonomy is limited
The MVP avoids a very large taxonomy on purpose. A smaller and more stable structure is more useful in the first stage because it improves consistency, reduces noise, and makes the analytical output easier to interpret.
