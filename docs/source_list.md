# Source list

## MVP source selection
The MVP uses a controlled set of seven public sources. The selection is intentionally limited in order to keep the first version of the project focused, interpretable, and methodologically consistent.

The source universe is divided intro two categories:

- official sources;
- think tanks.

This distinction is important because the project treats official communication separately from analytical interpretation.

## Official sources

### 1. Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Romania (MAE)
Role in the project:

Primary Romanian institutional source for official statements, visits, diplomatic positions, and regional cooperation developments.


### 2. NATO
Role in the project:

Core source for official communication related to the eastern flank, deterrence, regional security, and support for Ukraine.

### 3. Council of the European Union / European Council
Role in the project:

Source for formal EU-level conclusions, decisions, and strategic political positions relevant to regional security.

### 4. European External Action Service (EEAS)
Role in the project:

Source for the external dimension of EU action, especially regarding the eastern neighbourhood, Moldova, Ukraine, and wider regional security issues.

## Think tanks

### 5. European Council on Foreign Relations (ECFR)
Role in project:

Analytical source for EU-oriented interpretation of developments involving Moldova, Ukraine, and the wider eastern neighbourhood.

### 6. German Marshall Fund (GMF)
Role in the project:

Analytical source of regional security, Black Sea dynamics, and Romania-Moldova-Ukraine cooperation.

### 7. Carnegie Endowment for International Peace
Role in the project:

Analytical source for broader strategic interpretation, especially concerning Russia, Moldova, and regional geopolitical developments.

## Selection logic

The source list was build using four criteria:
1. Direct relevance to the analytical focus of the project;
2. Institutional or analytical credibility;
3. Regular publication of public material;
4. Compatibility with a traceable OSINT workflow based on open and lawful sources.

## Notes
The MVP does not include general news media or social media sources. This limitation in intentional. The first version of the project prioritizes source quality, traceabillity, and methodological control over source volume.
